## PowerPoint Implementation Instructions

- This document specifies **16 physical slides**. Slides marked (a) and (b) are
  separate slides — build both, hide the inapplicable version per audience
  using Slide > Hide Slide.
- Element labels (HEADLINE, SUBHEAD, BODY, CALLOUT, STAT BAR, SPEAKER NOTES)
  map to distinct text boxes. Do not merge into one block.
- SPEAKER NOTES on every slide include the audience tag [BANK], [CU], or [ALL]
  so the presenter knows at a glance whether to show the slide.
- No emoji anywhere in the deck — regulated industry product; content may appear
  in screen recordings or printed handouts reviewed by compliance staff.
- STAT BAR: three stats displayed side by side in a visually distinct horizontal
  band — not a bulleted list.
- Regulatory citations (31 USC §5318, §228.12(h)(2), etc.) appear in a smaller
  font CALLOUT box, not inline with body copy.
- CALLOUT BOX: visually distinct from body — use a border, background tint,
  or indented treatment.

## SLIDE 3a — The Problem [BANK]

AUDIENCE: BANK — hide for credit union presentations

HEADLINE:
Community banks carry a commercial bank's compliance burden —
with none of the resources.

THREE-COLUMN LAYOUT:

COLUMN 1 — BSA/AML:
ONE BSA OFFICER

Every bank must monitor transactions for suspicious activity and file Suspicious
Activity Reports with FinCEN within 30 days. At a community bank, this falls
on a single person.

The software they use was built for commercial banks — suburban branch networks,
middle-income customers, business account-heavy portfolios. Applied to an MDI,
it produces false positives. The BSA Officer spends their day closing alerts
on legitimate community transactions instead of investigating genuine
suspicious activity.

STAT: 60–70% of BSA Officer time lost to false positive investigation

COLUMN 2 — CRA:
ONE CRA OFFICER

CRA performance exams require documented evidence of lending, investment, and
community service activity — assembled, geocoded, validated, and formatted to
examiner standards.

Most institutions collect this evidence manually: spreadsheets, email threads,
Word documents pieced together in the 60–90 days before an examiner arrives.
The evidence already exists. The problem is capturing it.

STAT: 80–120 hours of staff time per exam cycle, most of it recoverable

COLUMN 3 — DIGITAL GAP:
ONE DIGITAL GAP

MDI customers increasingly expect the mobile banking and financial wellness
experience that larger institutions provide. Building it costs $200–500K
and 12+ months. Most community banks cannot absorb that investment.

STAT: $200–500K to build what Econofi deploys in weeks

BOTTOM (full width):
Econofi addresses all three — BSA/AML, CRA, and financial wellness —
in one platform, one implementation, one vendor relationship.

## SLIDE 4 — BSA/AML: TransactionMonitor [ALL]

AUDIENCE: ALL — show for both bank and credit union presentations

HEADLINE:
TransactionMonitor

SUBHEAD:
BSA/AML compliance built for community institution transaction patterns.
Every transaction screened nightly. Prioritized alerts with SAR narrative drafts
waiting for the BSA Officer each morning.

BODY — FOUR-STEP FLOW (numbered, left to right or top to bottom):

1. NIGHTLY BATCH
Every transaction — cash deposits, withdrawals, wires, ACH, checks — is exported
from your core system via secure SFTP. No changes to your core configuration.
Works with Fiserv, Jack Henry, and FIS standard exports.

2. PII SANITIZATION
All member or customer data is sanitized before AI screening. Account numbers
become SHA-256 hashes. Names become tokens. No raw PII reaches the AI layer,
the alert record, or any log.

3. PATTERN DETECTION
Four detection patterns run on every batch:
- Structuring — multiple sub-$10,000 deposits designed to avoid Currency Transaction Reports (31 USC §5324)
- Velocity anomaly — dormant accounts activating, sudden volume spikes vs. 6-month baseline
- Geographic risk — transactions involving FATF blacklist/greylist jurisdictions
- Round-dollar patterns — wire concentrations at exact amounts inconsistent with normal activity

4. ALERT QUEUE
Alerts sorted by severity — critical to low. Each alert includes the pattern detected,
transactions flagged, regulatory citation, SAR narrative draft, and recommended action.
The BSA Officer investigates and decides. TransactionMonitor does not file SARs.

CALLOUT BOX:
Every other BSA/AML platform was calibrated on commercial bank transaction populations.
Their baselines reflect suburban branch networks — not cash-primary communities where
a $4,000 deposit at three branches in one day is normal household behavior, not structuring.

TransactionMonitor's detection logic is built for community institution member behavior.
Fewer false positives. More time on genuine suspicious activity.

STAT BAR (three stats):
- Under 8 hours — alerts in queue from nightly batch
- 30 days — SAR filing deadline under 31 CFR §1020.320 (satisfied by nightly cycle)
- 0 — SARs filed without human authorization

REGULATORY CALLOUT (smaller font):
31 USC §5318(g) — Suspicious Activity Reporting
31 USC §5324 — Structuring
31 CFR §1020.320 — SAR filing requirements

SPEAKER NOTES:
[ALL] Show for both bank and credit union presentations. The regulatory citations
apply equally to banks and credit unions — FinCEN rules do not distinguish.
For credit union audiences, "customer" becomes "member" in verbal delivery.
