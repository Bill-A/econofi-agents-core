## History — Why Seaway Matters

Seaway National Bank of Chicago was **America's largest Black-owned bank** for decades. Founded in 1965, it served the South and West Sides of Chicago through 2017, when it was closed by the FDIC following capital shortfalls in the aftermath of the 2008 financial crisis.

Self-Help Federal Credit Union — one of the most established CDFI credit unions in the country — acquired Seaway's branches and deposits in May 2017, preserving the institution's community banking presence on the South Side rather than allowing the branches to disappear.

The Seaway name carries weight in Chicago's Black community that no other financial institution can claim. Daryl Newell, who served as Seaway's Chief Retail Banking Officer before the acquisition, has been running the division since August 2018. He knows the history, the community, and the compliance burden that comes with it.

This is not a prospect who needs to be educated on why MDI-calibrated banking technology matters. He lived through the institution's failure and its resurrection.

## The BSA/AML Angle for a Credit Union

The pitch is the same. The regulatory cite changes slightly.

Credit unions file SARs under **31 CFR §1020.320** (same as banks) and CTRs under **31 CFR §1010.311**. NCUA examines BSA/AML compliance during regular safety and soundness exams. A credit union BSA Officer has the same daily experience as a bank BSA Officer: too many alerts, too much time closing false positives, not enough time on genuine suspicious activity.

The MDI calibration argument is even stronger for Seaway. The South Side of Chicago is one of the most cash-intensive urban communities in the Midwest. A BSA/AML system calibrated for commercial bank transaction patterns will fire on Seaway member behavior constantly. Daryl Newell has seen this.

**One adjustment to the standard pitch**: Replace "bank" with "credit union" throughout. Do not cite CRA statutes. The regulatory stability argument for BSA/AML (31 USC §5318 since 1986, unchanged) stands fully.

## The Financial Wellness Angle

Self-Help Federal Credit Union's core mission is building wealth in underserved communities. Seaway's South Side and West Side Chicago membership is the target population for bilingual financial wellness tools — budgeting, financial education, savings programs.

The financial wellness product also generates structured community development service documentation — analogous to what CRA requires of banks but applicable to NCUA's community development credit union framework. Self-Help already operates as a Low-Income Credit Union (LICU) and Community Development Credit Union (CDCU). Documented member financial wellness activity supports both designations.

This is a mission sale, not a compliance sale — lead with what it does for members, not what it documents for regulators.
