## Mission and Market

Greenwood Archer Capital provides character-based, flexible financing for entrepreneurs in historically underinvested communities — primarily the South and West Sides of Chicago. Ninety percent of loans go to minority-owned businesses. Underwriting is relationship-driven: borrower reputation, track record, and community standing weigh alongside traditional credit metrics. No prepayment penalties; many loans available without collateral.

The name is a deliberate reference. Greenwood was the Tulsa neighborhood known as Black Wall Street. Archer Avenue runs through the heart of the South Side of Chicago. The brand signals both historical grounding and local identity.

## Econofi Product Fit Assessment

Greenwood Archer Capital is a **CDFI loan fund, not a chartered bank or credit union**. This distinction determines regulatory applicability and, therefore, which Econofi products apply.

### Product-by-Product Assessment

| Econofi Module | Applies to GAC? | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| BSA/AML TransactionMonitor | No — not at this stage | GAC does not hold deposits or process payment transactions at the volume or type that triggers CTR/SAR obligations under 31 USC §5318. Loan funds have lighter BSA exposure under 31 CFR §1029 than depository institutions. |
| CRA DataGuard | No | CRA is a bank regulation (12 CFR §228). Loan funds are not CRA-examined. |
| CRA NarrativeWriter | No | Same reason. |
| Fair Lending LoanDataAnalyzer | Yes — direct fit | ECOA (15 USC §1691) applies to all creditors regardless of charter type. A lender making 90% of loans to minority-owned businesses needs to demonstrate non-discriminatory pricing and terms across any non-minority applicants. Disparate impact exposure is real even at this portfolio size. |
| LIHTC/NMTC ComplianceMonitor | Unlikely at current scale | At $7.8M AUM, GAC is unlikely to be a tax credit equity investor. If they grow or begin acting as a CDE (Community Development Entity) for NMTC, this becomes relevant. |
| RegulatoryWatcher | Partial | CFPB/ECOA regulatory monitoring is relevant. |

### Bottom Line

At current scale and structure, **GAC is not a direct Econofi sales prospect**. The gap is entity type and size, not relationship quality or mission alignment.

## The Real Opportunity: Referral and Partnership

The value of the Terrence Johnson relationship is not a $25,000 BSA/AML subscription. It is access to the bank and CDFI network that funds and partners with GAC.

### 1. Bank Capital Partners (CRA Credit Seekers)

CDFIs like GAC are capitalized by banks seeking Community Reinvestment Act Community Development Test credit. Wintrust has a board seat on GAC's Loan Approval Committee. Other Chicago-area banks almost certainly provide capital to GAC as a CRA-qualifying investment or grant.

Those banks — particularly any MDI or CDFI-chartered depository institutions among them — are Econofi's target prospect. Terrence Johnson knows their compliance officers.

**Ask**: "Which banks provide capital or grant support to GAC? Are any of them MDIs or CDFI banks?"

### 2. CDFI Peer Network

Lisa Herrera (board) represents Raza Development Fund — a national CDFI loan fund with substantially more assets than GAC. The CDFI community is small and tightly networked. A warm introduction to a CDFI that has a banking charter (e.g., a CDFI bank or credit union) through the GAC board network is worth the relationship investment.

### 3. Arnold Pugh — Examiner Credibility

A retired Federal Reserve Bank examiner on GAC's board is an unusual asset for us. Pugh has likely seen hundreds of BSA/AML examinations and knows exactly what output BSA Officers need to produce to satisfy an examiner. A conversation with him — not a sales call, a legitimacy conversation — could inform product positioning and potentially yield an introduction to his successor network at the Fed.

**Ask Terrence**: "Would Arnold Pugh be willing to spend 30 minutes with us? We're building BSA/AML tooling for community banks and would value the perspective of someone who has sat on the examiner side."

### 4. If GAC Pursues a Banking Charter

Some CDFI loan funds pursue a banking charter as they scale — it unlocks deposit-taking, broadens capital sources, and changes the regulatory relationship. If that is on GAC's roadmap, the full Econofi compliance suite becomes relevant within 12–24 months of charter approval.

**Ask**: "Is GAC exploring a banking charter or credit union conversion?"

## Conversation Guide — First Follow-Up with Terrence Johnson

### Do not lead with product.

GAC is not a product prospect today. Leading with a pitch damages the relationship before it starts. Lead with mission alignment and curiosity.

### Opening frame

"We are building compliance automation for MDI and CDFI banks — specifically the BSA/AML and CRA burden that falls on a single compliance officer at a community institution. When we met, I wanted to learn more about GAC and whether there are institutions in your network who are dealing with that compliance weight."

### Questions to ask

1. "Who are your primary bank capital partners — the institutions that fund GAC's loan portfolio?"
2. "Are any of those banks MDIs or CDFI-certified depository institutions?"
3. "Do you have peers in the CDFI network who operate with a banking or credit union charter?"
4. "Is GAC thinking about a banking charter at any point?"
5. "Would Arnold Pugh be open to a brief conversation about BSA/AML exam expectations from the examiner's perspective?"

### What to leave behind

If Terrence asks what Econofi does: the one-paragraph version.

> "We built the first BSA/AML compliance agent designed specifically for MDIs and CDFI banks — not adapted from commercial bank software. It screens every transaction nightly and delivers a prioritized alert queue to the BSA Officer each morning, with SAR narrative drafts included. The detection logic is calibrated for MDI customer behavior — cash-primary communities, character-based lending patterns, community organization account activity — which reduces the false positive rate that burns BSA Officer time at commercial bank tools. We are in the pilot phase, working with institutions under $500M in assets."

Do not leave pricing. If asked, the BSA/AML subscription starts at $25,000/year for institutions under $200M. Frame it in the context of what a BSA Officer's time costs.
